Final season tables -- Queensland 1884 to 2023 -- Index

The list below sets out the champions (Grand Final winners) since 1959, and the premiership winner and runner-up for every season of top flight football in Queensland since it commenced in 1884.

 

Seasons 1884 to 1919

Prior to the establishment of the QFA in 1920, association football in Queensland was often loosely administered, and the level of reporting coverage varied from good to non-existent.

From 1884 to 1888 the AQFA held a Badges competition. After a hiatus, the QBFA succeeded the AQFA and commenced a Challenge Cup competition (also known in the media at the time as the Brisbane Challenge Cup) in 1892.

For seasons 1905, 1906, 1908, 1909, 1913, 1914, 1915, 1919, the Ipswich clubs did not affiliate with the QBFA.

Indicating the respective strength of clubs, Ipswich clubs won the QBFA Challenge Cup in 9 of 12 seasons from 1893 to 1904, so West Moreton British FA premiers are listed below (except 1906 in which no formal WMBFA competition appears to have occurred).

For 1884 to 1919, the final match listed below was sometimes a play-off match, and sometimes a deciding fixture which happened to feature the two top teams. The format of the Challenge Cup varied between seasons.

In the seasons leading up to the First World War, a league table was increasingly used to determine the premier team in both Brisbane and Ipswich, a play-off only required if the top teams shared equal points.

 

Seasons 1920 to current

For seasons 1920, 1925 and 1930 to 1936, the Ipswich championship was at least the equal of the Brisbane championship, and has been included below as a top flight league for these seasons.

During these seasons separate leagues were run by the Brisbane & District FA and the Ipswich West Moreton British FA due to a series of conflicts between the administrations.

 

For seasons 1962 and 1963, the Queensland Soccer Federation and the Queensland Soccer Football Association ran rival competitions, but only the QSF competition can be deemed a top flight league.

This is because of the ten clubs participating in 1961 Division One, eight joined the QSF. The QSF took over the control of all Queensland soccer after the 1963 Unity Series.

 

Champions have been determined by a final series contested in the top division of Queensland football for every season since 1959, except 2000.

Since 1920, premiers have been awarded every season to the team at the top of the table at the end of the regular season, except when decided by a Top Four final series in 1923, 1925, 1929, 1930, 1931 and 1933.
