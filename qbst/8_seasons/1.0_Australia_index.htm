Final season tables -- Australia 1977 to 2023 -- Index

The list below sets out the Grand Final result and premiership top four for every season of top flight football in Australia since it commenced in 1977.

 

For 1977 to 1983 and 1987, the championship was awarded to the team finishing at the top of the table. In some of these seasons a Top Four series was played, but it was not used to determine the champion team.

From 1984 to 1986 a conference system was used, with the champion team determined by a Grand Final between the final series winner in each conference.

Since 1988, in the NSL and subsequently in the A League, the champion team has always been decided by the Grand Final at the end of a final series.

In 2002-03, a championship series featuring the top six team took place after the regular season, followed by a Grand Final between the top two teams.

In 2004-05 there was no top flight football championship in Australia due to the transition from the NSL to the A League.

Since its inception, the A League has awarded the title of premiers to the team finishing top of the table at the end of the regular season, while the Grand Final winner has been awarded the title of champions.
