Note 1: Round 9 (29/06/1935) Merton Rovers played an unregistered player in their 2-1 win against Latrobe. The BDFA determined that the result stood, but awarded Latrobe two points and deducted two points from Merton Rovers.

Note 2: Round 13 (27/07/1935) Wynnum played an unregistered player in their 3-3 draw against Toombul United. The BDFA determined that the result stood, but awarded Toombul United one point and deducted one point from Wynnum (BDFA executive meeting 09/09/1935).

Note 3: Round 14 (03/08/1935) Toowong 0 Wynnum 5 was annulled and ordered to be replayed, because Wynnum fielded an ineligible player (BDFA executive meeting 09/09/1935).

The replayed game was scheduled for Raymond Park on 21 September 1935, but Toowong's ball burst during practice and Wynnum failed to bring a ball.

A further attempt to complete the game was scheduled for Lang Park on 28 September 1935. Toowong failed to appear, forfeited the game and was relegated.

Toowong was listed to appear in Division 3 in 1936, but there were not enough clubs to support a division so Toowong sat the 1936 season out.

Note 4: Latrobe-Milton (as the club was known from 1928 to 1940) fielded two teams in Division 1 from 1934 to 1938.
